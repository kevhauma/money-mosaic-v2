# TICKET-TXN-03 ‚Äî Manual attribution override for misattributed joint/personal expenses

- **Area:** Transactions
- **Type:** Feature
- **Traceability:** extends FR-TXN-1/FR-TXN-2; consumes [ACC-02](./TICKET-ACC-02-joint-ownership-share.md), [ACC-03](./TICKET-ACC-03-multi-owner-coowner-ibans.md), [STAT-03](./TICKET-STAT-03-contribution-net-worth.md); interacts with [TICKET-TRF-01](../../v1.0_foundation/tickets/TICKET-TRF-01-clear-category-on-link.md)'s link/unlink flow

## User story

As someone with a shared account, I want to correct a transaction that landed in the wrong 'personal vs. joint' bucket, so a one-off mistake (I fronted a joint expense from my own account, the joint account paid for something personal, or vice versa for a co-owner) doesn't distort my net worth or expense stats.

## Description

STAT-03 classifies every transaction's contribution to *my* net worth/expense stats purely from **which account it's on**: my own accounts count at 100%, a joint account's spend counts at my `ownershipShare` (`s`). That default is wrong for one-off mistakes ‚Äî a joint expense accidentally paid from my personal account, a personal expense accidentally paid from the joint account (mine or a co-owner's). This ticket adds a manual **attribution override** on any transaction so the user can correct the three misattribution scenarios, independent of which account the money actually moved through.

## Current situation (as-is)

- `Transaction` in [app-db.ts](../../../src/app/core/data-access/app-db.ts) (lines 19‚Äì37) has no field expressing "count this transaction differently than its account normally would" ‚Äî [STAT-03](./TICKET-STAT-03-contribution-net-worth.md)'s `classifyJointLeg` helper (not yet built) will decide `mineIn`/`mineOut`/`jointSpend`/`coOwnerIn` **solely** from account type + counterparty IBAN, with no per-transaction escape hatch.
- Manual category editing already exists ‚Äî [transaction-edit-form.component.ts](../../../src/app/feature-transactions/components/transaction-edit-form/transaction-edit-form.component.ts) emits a `TransactionEditResult` (`categoryId`, `categoryManual`, `notes`) via `TransactionsStore` ‚Äî but there's no attribution control alongside it, and no UI for it at all today.
- Transfers are linked/unlinked through [TransferLinkingService](../../../src/app/core/transfers/transfer-linking.service.ts) (`linkManually` / `unlink`), which also **clears any category** on both legs the moment they're linked (`performLink`'s `clearedCategory`, per [TICKET-TRF-01](../../v1.0_foundation/tickets/TICKET-TRF-01-clear-category-on-link.md)) ‚Äî so a linked transfer leg can never itself carry the category needed to explain "this reimburses a specific joint expense."
- Nothing in the app today lets a joint-account outflow be pinned to "100% mine" or "0% mine" instead of the default `s`; nothing lets an own-account outflow be pinned to a joint account's share instead of the default 100%.

## Desired result (to-be)

- `Transaction` gains an optional `attributionOverride?: { mode: 'personal' | 'shared' | 'notMine'; jointAccountId?: number; reimbursementTransferId?: number }`. Meaning per mode:
  - `personal` ‚Äî this transaction counts **100%** toward my net worth/expense stats, regardless of account. (Scenario: a personal-only expense accidentally paid from the joint account.)
  - `notMine` ‚Äî this transaction counts **0%**. (Scenario: a co-owner's personal-only expense paid from the joint account ‚Äî not shared with me at all.)
  - `shared` ‚Äî this transaction is weighted by `jointAccountId`'s `ownershipShare` instead of its own account's default weighting. `jointAccountId` is **required** in this mode whenever the user has more than one joint account (disambiguates whose share applies); with exactly one joint account it can be inferred. (Scenario: a joint/shared expense accidentally paid from my own personal account.)
- **`shared` + a reimbursement**: when the flagged own-account expense was reimbursed by the joint account (the common "I fronted it, the pot paid me back" case ‚Äî three transactions: my account `‚àíX` to the merchant, joint account `‚àíX` to me, my account `+X` from joint, with the last two already linked as a normal [Transfer](../../../src/app/core/data-access/app-db.ts)), the user additionally points the override at that transfer via `reimbursementTransferId` (picked from a short list of the account's linked transfers within the matching window, or by selecting the transfer directly). Once set, **both legs of that transfer are excluded from net worth and income/expense stats** (their contribution becomes 0) ‚Äî the expense's own `shared`-weighted amount is the only thing that counts. This keeps net worth and the expense-stat category breakdown consistent with each other; see Notes for why the alternative (reclassifying the transfer's joint leg instead) was rejected.
  - **Why auto-suppress via the picked link rather than reclassifying the transfer:** reclassifying the transfer's joint leg to `jointSpend@share` fixes net worth but leaves the original expense line still reporting its **full** amount in expense/category stats (own-account transactions are always 100%, unweighted) ‚Äî net worth and the expense stat would then disagree with each other on the same event. Suppressing the linked transfer and weighting only the flagged expense line keeps both figures consistent, at the cost of the transfer's `(1 ‚àí s)` share of real cash movement not being individually attributed to anyone ‚Äî an extension of the already-accepted "informal IOU" gap (see STAT-03's Notes), not a new problem.
  - `shared` **without** a `reimbursementTransferId` is valid (I fronted a joint expense and was never paid back): only the expense line is weighted at `s`; nothing is suppressed. The `(1 ‚àí s)` portion permanently doesn't show up in either raw balance-vs-stake gap or net worth ‚Äî same accepted trade-off, now visible on a personal account for the first time (see Notes).
- The transaction edit UI ([transaction-edit-form.component.ts](../../../src/app/feature-transactions/components/transaction-edit-form/transaction-edit-form.component.ts) + template) gains an **attribution** control, shown only when at least one joint account exists: a mode picker (`Default` / `Personal (100%)` / `Shared (my share)` / `Not mine (0%)`), a joint-account picker when `shared` is chosen and more than one joint account exists, and ‚Äî only for `shared` ‚Äî an optional "reimbursed by a transfer" picker.
- `STAT-03`'s classification helper reads `attributionOverride` **before** falling back to its default account-based rule, so `netWorth`, `computeNetWorthTrend`, `computePeriodStats`, and `computeCategoryBreakdown` all honour it identically (single source of truth, per STAT-03's own "no duplicated per-site logic" requirement).
- Scenario 3 (a co-owner reimburses **my** personal expense by depositing into the joint account) needs **no override at all** ‚Äî it already lands correctly as that co-owner's `neutral`/`coOwnerIn` contribution (0% to my stake, excluded from my income) via [CAT-02](./TICKET-CAT-02-neutral-category-kind.md) + STAT-03. This ticket adds regression test coverage locking that in, not a new mechanism.

- [x] Setting an override never mutates the account-default classification of *other* transactions, and never touches a transaction's raw `amount` ‚Äî all weighting stays in the derived/computed layer, consistent with STAT-03's "no stored amount is ever mutated" rule.

## Notes

- **Worked example** (`s = 0.5`, groceries `X = ‚Ç¨100` fronted from my checking account, reimbursed in full by the joint account): without an override, my checking account nets `‚àí100 + 100 = 0` (real cash, unweighted) and the joint account's reimbursement leg is a plain `mineOut` at 100%, so net worth drops by the full `‚Ç¨100` and the expense-stat category breakdown shows `‚Ç¨100` under groceries. With the override (`shared`, `jointAccountId` = the joint account, `reimbursementTransferId` = the reimbursement transfer): the checking `‚àí100` line is weighted at `s` (contributes `‚àí‚Ç¨50`), the transfer's two legs are excluded entirely, and expense/net worth both agree on `‚Ç¨50`.
- **Why `shared` needs an explicit `jointAccountId`, not "the" joint account:** a user can have more than one joint account (e.g. one with a partner, one with a parent) with different shares ‚Äî inferring the wrong one would silently misweight the expense. Require it whenever ambiguous; auto-fill when there's exactly one.
- **Consequence worth documenting in the UI (not just this ticket):** once `shared`/`personal`/`notMine` exist, a personal account's net-worth "stake" can diverge from its raw balance for the first time ‚Äî previously only joint accounts had that gap (STAT-03's accepted "informal IOU" trade-off). Same trade-off, now reachable on any account via an explicit user action rather than the account's type alone.
- **Scope guard:** this is *not* the general "split one transaction across multiple categories" feature parked in the v2 backlog ([../v2/requirements.md](../../v2/requirements.md) ‚Äî "split transactions"). `attributionOverride` reweights a transaction's contribution as a whole; it never assigns more than one category to it.
- Depends on [ACC-02](./TICKET-ACC-02-joint-ownership-share.md) (the share being overridden to), [ACC-03](./TICKET-ACC-03-multi-owner-coowner-ibans.md) (to tell a co-owner's personal spend apart, for the `notMine` scenario), and [STAT-03](./TICKET-STAT-03-contribution-net-worth.md) (the classification helper this plugs into). Build last in the v1.1 sequence.
